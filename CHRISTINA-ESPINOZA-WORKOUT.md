# Christina Espinoza, 5 Day Muscle Building Program

Intermediate level, hypertrophy focus, with 2 dedicated leg days plus 1 easy run day and a short jog finisher on push day. Each session lands around 55 to 65 minutes.

## Weekly split

- **Day 1 (Mon):** Lower, glute and hamstring bias
- **Day 2 (Tue):** Upper push, chest, shoulders, triceps
- **Day 3 (Wed):** Easy long run plus core
- **Day 4 (Thu):** Lower, quad bias
- **Day 5 (Fri):** Upper pull, back, biceps, rear delts
- **Sat / Sun:** Rest

Run day is sandwiched between the two leg days on purpose. Glutes and hams worked Monday, easy aerobic run Wednesday (mostly quads / calves, no strength stimulus), then quad day Thursday on rested legs. She gets a full 3 day window of recovery before Monday again.

## Running plan (Day 3)

Zone 2, conversational pace. She should be able to hold a full sentence but not sing. Heart rate roughly 60 to 70 percent of max. Progress like this:

- **Weeks 1 to 2:** 30 min continuous
- **Weeks 3 to 4:** 35 min
- **Weeks 5 to 6:** 40 min
- **Weeks 7 to 8:** 45 min
- **Weeks 9+:** hold 45 min, start nudging pace (still conversational but the lower end of harder)

If she misses a week, drop back one step. Outdoor is fine, treadmill at 1 percent incline is fine.

## DAY 1, LOWER, GLUTE AND HAMSTRING

**Warm up (about 8 min)**

1. Stairmaster, easy pace, 5 min
2. Intermediate Hip Flexor and Quad Stretch, 30s each side
3. Resistance Band Hip Thrust on Knees, 15 reps (glute activation)
4. Bodyweight squat, 10 reps (feeler)

**Main work**

1. **Smith Machine Romanian Deadlift** | 3 working sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90s rest
   - Notes: Do 2 ramp up sets first to find working weight. Push hips back, feel the stretch in the hamstrings, do not round the lower back. Stop the bar around mid shin, no need to touch the floor.

2. **Barbell Hip Thrust** | 4 sets x 8 to 12 reps | 90 to 120s rest
   - Notes: Heaviest lift of the day. Drive through the heels, ribs down, full lockout with a hard glute squeeze at the top. Pause 1 second at the top of every rep.

3. **Superset: Standing Hip Abduction + Sliding Reverse Lunge Machine** | 3 rounds | 60s rest between rounds
   - Abduction: 12 to 15 reps, slow tempo, no leaning
   - Sliding Reverse Lunge: 10 reps per leg, controlled stretch into the lunge
   - Notes: This is where you get the burn. Do not race. Quality over speed.

4. **Seated Leg Curl Machine** | 3 sets x 10 to 12 reps | 60s rest
   - Notes: 3 second eccentric on every rep. Squeeze the hamstrings hard at the bottom. Knees a little past the pad for a deeper stretch.

5. **Cable Hip Hinge** | 2 sets x 12 to 15 reps | 45s rest
   - Notes: Finisher. Smooth and controlled, feel the glutes and hams loading on every rep.

**Cool down (about 5 min)**

- Standing Straight Leg Hamstring Stretch, 30s each side
- Pigeon Glutes Stretch, 45s each side
- Child Pose Lower Back Stretch, 45s

---

## DAY 2, UPPER PUSH, CHEST, SHOULDERS, TRICEPS

**Warm up (about 8 min)**

1. Elliptical, easy pace, 5 min
2. Arm Circle, 10 each direction
3. Resistance Band Face Pull, 15 reps (rear delt and rotator cuff prep)
4. Push up, 8 reps from knees if needed (feeler)

**Main work**

1. **Chest Press Machine** | 4 sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90s rest
   - Notes: Do 2 lighter feeler sets first to groove the pattern. Pause 1 second at the bottom of every rep with a full stretch on the pecs.

2. **Incline Bench Cable Fly** | 3 sets x 12 reps | 75s rest
   - Notes: Set the bench at about 30 degrees. Slight elbow bend, lock it in, then open up wide. Feel the stretch across the upper chest. Do not let the cables snap you back.

3. **Iso Lateral Shoulder Press Both Arms** | 3 sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90s rest
   - Notes: Bench upright, press straight up, do not flare elbows out. Stop just short of lockout to keep tension on the delts.

4. **Superset: Cbum Raises + Cable Rear Delt Fly** | 3 rounds | 60s rest between rounds
   - Cbum Raises: 12 to 15 reps, lean slightly away from the working side, lead with the elbow
   - Cable Rear Delt Fly: 12 to 15 reps, think about pulling the cables apart with the rear shoulders, not the arms
   - Notes: Light weight, strict form. Side and rear delts are tiny muscles, no ego.

5. **Overhead Seated Dumbbell Extension** | 3 sets x 10 to 12 reps | 60s rest
   - Notes: Use one dumbbell with both hands. Get a deep stretch at the bottom, that is where the triceps grow. Elbows tight, do not flare.

**Cool down (about 5 min)**

- Rear Deltoid Stretch, 30s each side
- Child Pose Arms Extended Left Right, 45s
- Across body shoulder stretch, 30s each side

---

## DAY 3, EASY RUN AND CORE

**Warm up (about 5 min)**

- Brisk walk on treadmill at 3.0 to 3.5 mph, 5 min

**Main work**

1. **Running** | 30 to 45 min depending on the week (see running plan above)
   - Notes: Conversational pace the whole time. If she cannot talk in full sentences, slow down. The point is aerobic base building, not punishment. If outside, pick a flat route. If treadmill, set incline to 1 percent.

2. **Core circuit, 3 rounds, 45s rest between rounds**
   - Hanging Knee Raises, 10 to 12 reps (controlled, no swinging)
   - Plank on Elbows, 45 to 60 seconds
   - Side Plank, 30 seconds each side
   - Notes: If hanging knee raises are too hard, sub Plank Knee Tucks. Keep the abs braced the whole time.

**Cool down (about 5 min)**

- Standing Bow Hamstring Stretch, 30s each side
- Pigeon Glutes Stretch, 45s each side
- Intermediate Hip Flexor and Quad Stretch, 30s each side

---

## DAY 4, LOWER, QUAD BIAS

**Warm up (about 8 min)**

1. Stairmaster, easy pace, 5 min
2. Alternating Leg Downward Dog, 8 reps each side
3. Bodyweight squat, 10 reps (feeler, slow tempo)
4. Bodyweight reverse lunge, 6 reps per leg

**Main work**

1. **Pendulum Squat** | 4 working sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90 to 120s rest
   - Notes: Do 2 ramp up sets first. Full range of motion, sit deep, drive up through the midfoot. This is the big lift of the day, give it the most effort.

2. **Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squat** | 3 sets x 10 reps per leg | 75s rest between legs
   - Notes: Front foot far enough out that the front knee tracks over the foot. Slight forward lean to load the glutes a bit more, mostly quad work. Slow on the way down.

3. **Lever Leg Extension** | 4 sets x 12 to 15 reps | 60s rest
   - Notes: 1 second pause at the top with a hard quad squeeze. 3 second eccentric. Last set is a hard burnout, push to 1 or 2 reps from failure.

4. **Superset: Standing Hip Thrust + Standing Calf Raises Smith Machine** | 3 rounds | 60s rest between rounds
   - Standing Hip Thrust: 10 to 12 reps, full glute squeeze at the top
   - Calf Raises: 12 to 15 reps, full stretch at the bottom, 1 second pause at the top
   - Notes: Calves need full range. Do not bounce.

5. **Hanging Knee Raises** | 3 sets x 10 to 12 reps | 45s rest
   - Notes: Finisher. Controlled, no momentum.

**Cool down (about 5 min)**

- All Fours Quad Stretch, 30s each side
- Standing Straight Leg Hamstring Stretch, 30s each side
- Child Pose, 45s

---

## DAY 5, UPPER PULL, BACK, BICEPS, REAR DELTS

**Warm up (about 8 min)**

1. Elliptical, easy pace, 5 min
2. Resistance Band Face Pull, 15 reps
3. Arm Circle Backward, 10 reps
4. Light lat pulldown, 12 reps (feeler weight)

**Main work**

1. **Cable Close Grip Front Lat Pulldown** | 4 sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90s rest
   - Notes: Do 1 to 2 feeler sets first. Pull elbows down and back, drive them into your sides. Chest up, slight backward lean. Feel the lats, not just the arms.

2. **Chest Supported Plate Loaded T Bar Row** | 3 sets x 8 to 10 reps | 90s rest
   - Notes: Pull to the lower chest, squeeze the mid back hard at the top, controlled stretch on the way down. Chest stays planted on the pad the entire set.

3. **Single Arm Incline Bench Lat Pulldown** | 3 sets x 10 to 12 reps per arm | 60s rest between arms
   - Notes: Reach all the way up at the top for a full lat stretch. Pull the elbow down toward the hip, not the chest. This one is gold for lat development.

4. **Superset: Cable Face Pull With Rope + Dumbbell Hammer Curl** | 3 rounds | 60s rest between rounds
   - Face Pull: 12 to 15 reps, pull to the forehead, externally rotate at the end
   - Hammer Curl: 10 to 12 reps, both arms together, no swinging
   - Notes: Face pulls are for rear delts and posture. Light weight, strict.

5. **Barbell Bicep Curl Normal Grip** | 3 sets x 8 to 10 reps | 60s rest
   - Notes: Elbows pinned to the sides. No body english on the way up. Slow on the way down, 2 second eccentric.

**Cool down (about 5 min)**

- Single Arm Lat Stretch, 30s each side
- Child Pose Lower Back Stretch, 45s
- Cross body shoulder stretch, 30s each side

## Programming notes for you (the coach)

- **Volume:** Each muscle group gets between 10 and 16 hard sets per week, which is the sweet spot for an intermediate woman building muscle. Glutes get hit on Day 1, Day 3 (running carries some), and Day 4. Quads get Day 4 plus the running. Hams get Day 1 plus running carryover.
- **Progression:** Add 1 rep per set until she hits the top of the rep range on every set, then bump weight 5 percent and reset to the bottom of the range. Standard double progression.
- **Why no leg press AND hack squat AND pendulum together:** Pendulum Squat is her one bilateral squat pattern. Bulgarian is unilateral so it gives a different stimulus. Leg Extension is pure isolation. That covers the quads without three machines doing the same thing.
- **Why no Nordic curls on hamstring day:** She is intermediate, not advanced. Nordics are brutal and would wreck her for the rest of the week. Seated leg curl gives plenty of stimulus.
- **Run day placement:** Wednesday on purpose. If you move the run to a day adjacent to a leg day, she will either run on tired quads or train legs on tired quads. Wednesday gives her clean recovery in both directions.
- **If she wants a second short run:** Add 15 to 20 min easy after Day 2 (push day). Do NOT add it on a leg day or the day before a leg day.
- **Total weekly time:** About 5 hours including warm ups and cool downs.
