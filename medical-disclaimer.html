Before You Start

You should consult your physician before beginning any new exercise or nutrition program. This is especially important if you:

  • are pregnant, postpartum, or trying to conceive
  • have any heart condition, history of cardiovascular disease, or family history of sudden cardiac events
  • have high or low blood pressure
  • have diabetes or any metabolic condition
  • have asthma or any respiratory condition
  • have a current or past eating disorder, disordered eating pattern, or significant unintentional weight change
  • have a history of depression, anxiety, or any mental-health condition
  • have any musculoskeletal injury, joint problem, or recent surgery
  • take any prescription or over-the-counter medication
  • are under 18 or over 65
  • have been sedentary for an extended period
  • feel any uncertainty about whether exercise is safe for you
STOP EXERCISING AND SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION if you experience chest pain, pressure in the chest, jaw or arm pain, shortness of breath disproportionate to the activity, dizziness, fainting, nausea, severe headache, blurred vision, severe joint pain, or any other symptom that could indicate medical distress. IN AN EMERGENCY, CALL YOUR LOCAL EMERGENCY NUMBER (in the US, 911) — do not message your Coach, do not wait for support, and do not log it in the app first.

Assumption of Risk

Exercise and changes to diet or lifestyle carry inherent risks. These risks include but are not limited to muscle strains, joint injuries, ligament or tendon tears, falls, bruising, dehydration, heat illness, equipment-related injury, abnormal blood-pressure response, fainting, cardiovascular events up to and including heart attack and stroke, and in rare cases serious injury or death. Dietary changes and caloric restriction carry their own risks, including nutritional deficiencies, gastrointestinal distress, hormonal disruption, and disordered eating patterns. Mental Health and Disordered Eating

If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm, an active eating disorder, or any mental-health crisis, please contact a crisis line in your country (in the US, call or text 988; in the UK, call 116 123 for Samaritans; in the EU, see findahelpline.com) — do not rely on Ziquecoach or your Coach for crisis support.
